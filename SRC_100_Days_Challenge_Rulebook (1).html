§1

Welcome

Welcome to the Sunday Run Club (SRC) 100-Day Challenge — 2nd Edition. This challenge is designed to inspire consistency, build lifelong fitness habits, strengthen our community and encourage participants to become healthier together through friendly team competition.

"Keep showing up. Every day counts."

§2

Objectives

  • Build consistency over 100 days.
  • Encourage an active lifestyle.
  • Foster teamwork and accountability.
  • Celebrate effort, discipline and participation.
  • Make fitness enjoyable for everyone.
§3

Registration & Eligibility

  • Register before the challenge begins.
  • Registration is valid after confirmation.
  • Organizers may verify details where required.
§4

Team Formation

Balanced teams will be formed by the Organizing Committee. Team allocation is final. Organizers may make changes in exceptional situations to ensure fairness.

§5

Team Captains

Captains motivate their teams, share announcements, review activity logs, resolve minor issues, encourage sportsmanship and escalate matters to the Organizing Committee.

Appeals

Appeals against a captain's decision may be made within 48 hours.

§6

Eligible Activities

  • Running
  • Walking
  • Cycling
  • Swimming
  • Strength Training
  • Yoga
  • Cricket
  • Football
  • Badminton
  • Pickleball
  • Tennis
  • Squash
  • Trekking
  • Dance Fitness & other recognised activities
Not eligible

Routine daily chores are not eligible. Multiple eligible activities may be performed in a day and the total duration will count for scoring.

§7

Activity Logging

Log all activities through the official portal. GPS-recorded activities are preferred. Supporting evidence may be requested for manual entries.

Delayed logging

Delayed logging is permitted. Activity points and team score remain unaffected. A −1 penalty applies only to the Logging Consistency Streak for each delayed day.

§8

Scoring

A duration-based scoring system will be used. The official scoring table will be published separately and forms an integral part of this Rulebook.

§10

Special Recognition

The Organizing Committee may introduce additional award categories or special recognition awards during the course of the challenge to celebrate exceptional performances, outstanding sportsmanship, remarkable consistency, leadership, community contribution, breakthrough achievements, or any accomplishment that embodies the true spirit of the Sunday Run Club.

§11

Fair Play

Integrity

Honesty and integrity are the foundation of the challenge. Suspicious activities may be reviewed. False or manipulated records may result in warnings, point deductions, rejection, suspension or disqualification.

§12

Health & Safety

Participants are responsible for ensuring they are medically fit to undertake physical activity. The Sunday Run Club, Team Captains and Organizing Committee shall not be liable for any injury, illness, accident or loss arising from participation in the challenge.

§13

Organizer Authority

The Organizing Committee shall interpret these rules and resolve disputes in the interest of fairness, transparency and the spirit of the challenge. The Committee reserves the right to review any activity that appears unrealistic or inconsistent with the spirit of the challenge and may request supporting evidence. The Committee's decision shall be final.

§14

The Spirit of the Challenge

Whether you walk, run, swim, lift, cycle, play —
or simply show up every day...

You are building something far greater than fitness.

Discipline. Consistency. Confidence.
